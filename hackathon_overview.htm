About the Hackathon

Where: SOFWERX, 1320 E. 9th Ave., Tampa, FL 33605

When: May 21st - 22nd 2016

Theme: Human-Machine Collaboration

How: Teams at various locations

This year's exciting theme, Human-Machine Collaboration, is part of the overall, highly ambitious Third Offset Strategy.

Building on the success of the October 2015 Hackathon at SOFWERX, we have new challenges all with a focus on supporting the Warfighter. We've worked closely with U.S. Special Operations Command, U.S. Central Command and other organizations across Tampa Bay to develop Hackathon challenges that support the Mil-OSS goals of improving use of the taxpayers' funds through requirements extraction, wise execution, and streamlined delivery by leveraging open source and open technology innovation.

  • Upshot (document translation)
  • BoxBotix (UAV Sensor)
  • Rocky (Cyber)
  • Batman (Locating Objects)

Location

This event will also take place at the Mil-OSS Home-base of SOFWERX in Tampa. And, just like last October, we'll be donating all remaining funds after expenses to the Special Operations Warrior Foundation.

Awards

Donations go toward, among other things, excellent prizes. We haven't yet selected this year's awesome prizes but last year's winning teams and individuals received drones. All participants must attend the event kickoff and the closing presentations at their respective locations to be eligible for awards.

Dress Code

As this is an open source software event and out of respect for the developers, casual attire is expected. For those who arrive wearing a coat, t-shirts are available at the door.

Registration

Registration is free for this event but registration closes May 19st at 5:00p.m.

Teams

You can be a Team of One.

As part of a team, you can serve as a Designer, Hacker, or Hustler.

In addition to teams participating from SOFWERX we will also have teams participating simultaneously from The Wright Brothers Institute and from The Griffiss Instutute.Teams may work from one of the official event locations or a location of their choosing. Each location will be linked via video.

Questions?

Contact Jeff Young or the Hackathon steering committee for more information.
